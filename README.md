# Employee Attrition Analysis – Green Destinations

##  Project Overview
Employee attrition is a critical challenge for organizations as it impacts productivity, costs, and workforce stability.  
This project analyzes employee attrition data from **Green Destinations**, a travel agency, to identify key factors influencing employee turnover.

The goal is to derive **data-driven insights** that can help HR teams improve retention strategies.

---

##  Objectives
- Calculate the overall employee attrition rate  
- Analyze how factors such as **Age**, **Years at Company**, and **Monthly Income** affect attrition  
- Identify patterns that can support HR decision-making  

---

##  Dataset Description
The dataset contains **1,470 employee records** with attributes such as:
- Demographics: Age, Gender, Marital Status  
- Job-related features: Department, Job Role, Job Level  
- Compensation: Monthly Income, Daily Rate  
- Experience: Years at Company, Total Working Years  
- Satisfaction metrics: Job Satisfaction, Work-Life Balance  
- Target variable: **Attrition (Yes / No)**

---

##  Methodology
1. **Data Cleaning & Preparation**
   - Checked for missing values and inconsistencies  
   - Converted categorical variables for analysis  

2. **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**
   - Calculated overall attrition rate  
   - Visualized attrition trends by age, income, department, and experience  

3. **Insight Generation**
   - Identified high-risk attrition groups  
   - Derived actionable insights for employee retention  

---

##  Key Findings
- Overall attrition rate is approximately **16%**
- Younger employees (20–30 years) show higher attrition  
- Employees with fewer years at the company are more likely to leave  
- Lower monthly income correlates with higher attrition  
- Overtime and poor work-life balance increase attrition risk  

---

##  Conclusion
The analysis reveals that **age, income, experience, and work-life balance** play a significant role in employee attrition.  
Targeted HR strategies focusing on early-career employees and compensation structures can significantly reduce attrition rates.
